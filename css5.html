Top 5 astronauts who spent the longest time in space on a single mission

sunita williams

Sunita Williams is an American astronaut and a former United States Navy officer of Indian-Slovenian descent. She was born on September 19, 1965, in Euclid, Ohio, USA. Her father, Deepak Pandya, is of Indian origin from Gujarat, and her mother, Ursuline Bonnie Pandya, is of Slovenian descent. Sunita grew up in Needham, Massachusetts, and attended Needham High School. She earned a Bachelor of Science degree in physical science from the United States Naval Academy in 1987 and a Master of Science degree in engineering management from Florida Institute of Technology in 1995.

kalpana chawal

Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut and the first woman of Indian origin to go to space. She was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India. From a young age, Kalpana was fascinated by the sky and dreamed of flying. She completed her schooling in Karnal and later pursued a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College in Chandigarh. In 1982, she moved to the United States to continue her studies. She earned a Master of Science degree in aerospace engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington and later completed her Ph.D. in aerospace engineering from the University of Colorado Boulder.

butch wilmore

Barry Eugene "Butch" Wilmore is an American astronaut and a United States Navy captain. He was born on December 29, 1962, in Murfreesboro, Tennessee, USA. Wilmore attended Mt. Juliet High School in Mt. Juliet, Tennessee. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in electrical engineering from Tennessee Technological University and later obtained a Master of Science degree in aviation systems from the University of Tennessee.

valeri polyakov

Valeri Polyakov was a Russian cosmonaut and medical doctor who holds the record for the longest single spaceflight in history. He spent 437 days and 18 hours aboard the Mir space station from 1994 to 1995. His mission was crucial for studying the effects of long-duration space travel on the human body, providing valuable insights for future missions to Mars and beyond.

sergi avdeyev

Sergei Avdeyev is a retired Russian cosmonaut and physicist known for spending an impressive amount of time in space. He held the record for the longest cumulative time in space at 747 days, 14 hours, and 14 minutes across three missions aboard the Mir space station in the 1990s.
